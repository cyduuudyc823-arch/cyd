Project Case Study

Impact Connect: Rebuilding Trust & Engagement for Gen Z in Charity

How might we win back the trust of younger generations through community co-creation and value exchange?

Context

Gen Z is becoming a social force, but their trust in traditional charities is declining. They reject one-way, passive donations and demand meaningful participation.

My Role

Service Design Researcher, Strategy Specialist, Prototyping Lead.

Collaborators

Red Cross

WWF

Songbird Survival

The Challenge

From Bystanders to Participants

Our core challenge was to transform "donation" into an "experience" without increasing the operational burden on charities. We needed to bridge the gap between financial support and active engagement.

Key Insight

Rewarded Altruism

Gen Z doesn't want a collection box; they want a platform for growth. Research revealed they value "self-actualization" as much as moral satisfaction. We designed for "Mutual Value Exchange."

Strategy Pivot

Empowerment over Creation

Instead of creating a new intermediary organization, we focused on **Empowering existing communities.** By leveraging university clubs and interest groups, we lowered system complexity and barriers to entry.
